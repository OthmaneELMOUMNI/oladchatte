The Ancestry

Born of the Desert,
Carried by Love

In 1895, a journey began across the shifting sands of the Tata region. It was here that Chatta, a master of desert poetry and resonance, laid the foundations of what would become a generational legacy.

Today, Oulad Chatta stands as the quintessential vessel for the Sahrawi spirit. Their music is not merely performance; it is a ritual of memory, an echo of the wind through the acacia trees, and the pulse of the Saharan night.

"The sand remembers every song we never finished."
